Q1 · THE FILM

Lessons live on the timeline, not in a doc.

The board replays the incident minute by minute. When the room spots a lesson, it gets pinned to the exact moment it happened — so "we should have caught this at 14:09" stays attached to 14:09 forever.

  1. 14:02
    Deploy 508 ships a config change to the queue workers
  2. 14:09
    First alert fires — but only in the muted #infra-noise channel
    LESSON · PINNED 14:09Queue-depth alerts route to a muted channel. Move them to #on-call with paging. → became Action 2
  3. 14:31
    Rollback of 508 · queue drains, checkout recovers
  4. 14:40
    All clear — total customer impact 38 minutes
    LESSON · PINNED 14:40Rollback was 4 minutes once decided; the delay was deciding. Pre-authorize rollback for queue regressions. → became Action 4

Blameless, not toothless.

The film describes systems and moments, not culprits: "the alert routed to a muted channel", never "Sipho missed the alert". Blame changes what people are willing to say in the room, and the board is only as good as what gets said.

The AI drafts the narrative from the timeline — every sentence linked back to a timestamp — and the facilitator signs it before it becomes the record. Nothing enters the archive unsigned.

Q2 · THE ACTIONS

A lesson without an owner is a wish.

Every pinned lesson must convert into an action with a named owner and a due date — or be explicitly declined, with the reason on the board. The repeat counter keeps everyone honest about which is which.
